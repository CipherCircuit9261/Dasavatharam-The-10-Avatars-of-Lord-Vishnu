Chakrathazhwar


In Hinduism Vishnu posseses four symbols on his four hands.They are the discus Chakratazhwar Chakra, the bow Sharanga, the mace Kaumodaki, the conch Panchajanya, and the sword Nandaka. His most powerful and recognisable weapons is the Chakratazhwar Chakaram. However, in actuality this chakram is Chakrathazhwar, also known as Chakratazhwar Perumal. He emerges as the divine embodiment of Vishnu's most potent weapon the sudharshana chakram, signifying the guide to enlightenment. Found in dedicated shrines across Vishnu-associated temples, Chakrathazhwar is believed to embody the combined grace of Varaha and Narasimha. Chakrathazhwar is one among the nithya sooriyas who perform kainkaryam to the divya dampathis for eternity. Chakratazhwar has appeared countless times throughout the four yugas.
